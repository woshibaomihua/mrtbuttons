How Much Do Garment Buttons Cost?

Most factories answer this with “please inquire”. Here are the actual ex-works ranges from our own 843-style catalogue, what moves them, and a worked cost example for a 5,000-piece order.

Short answer: most garment buttons run $0.02–$0.36 per piece ex-works Wenzhou, decided mainly by material. Resin shirt buttons are cheapest at about $0.02–$0.06; zinc-alloy metal buttons with an engraved logo run $0.06–$0.36. Standard MOQ is 1,000 pieces per colour and size, and many stock styles start at 100.

What MOQ actually means here

MOQ is counted per colour and per size, which is the detail that surprises most first-time buyers. Three colours of the same button is three MOQs, not one.

Order typeQuantityWhat it is for
Stock / small lot100–300 pcsConfirming colour, weight and feel before committing
Standard production MOQ1,000 pcs per colour & sizeMost production orders
Volume10,000 pcs and upRepeat programmes, best unit price

The five things that actually move your price

  1. Material. This is the single biggest lever — the spread between resin and plated metal is roughly 6× at the same quantity.
  2. Plating and finish. Standard nickel or gold plating sits at the bottom of the range; antique, matte, gun-metal and enamel-fill finishes add process steps and cost.
  3. Logo method. Laser engraving carries no tooling charge. A 3D embossed logo needs a mold, which is a one-time $40–80 depending on complexity.
  4. Size (ligne). A 32L coat button uses far more material than an 18L shirt button. Our range runs 14L–60L.
  5. Quantity. Our own listings show a 3–6× spread between the MOQ price and the volume price on the same style.

Six ways to bring the cost down without cutting quality

  1. Consolidate colours. Since MOQ is per colour, dropping from four colourways to two halves your minimum commitment.
  2. Stay on standard ligne sizes. Non-standard sizes can mean new tooling.
  3. Use laser engraving for short runs. It skips the mold fee entirely; save the 3D mold for styles you will reorder.
  4. Take standard plating where the design allows, and put the budget into the buttons the customer actually sees.
  5. Buy the year in one PO and schedule releases. You get the volume tier without warehousing it all at once.
  6. Ask which stock styles fit. Stock styles ship from 100 pcs and skip sampling time altogether.

Frequently asked questions

What is the minimum order for custom buttons?

1,000 pieces per colour and size for custom production. Many stock styles are available from 100 pieces, which is the usual route for sampling or a capsule run.

How much does a button mold cost?

A one-time $40–80 for a 3D embossed logo mold, depending on complexity. Laser-engraved logos need no mold at all.

Are samples free?

Stock samples are free. Custom samples take 5–7 days and we confirm the cost with you before starting, since it depends on the tooling involved.

Why do metal buttons cost more than resin ones?

Three reasons stack up: the raw zinc alloy or brass costs more than polyester resin, plating is a separate process step, and metal styles more often carry an embossed logo that needs a mold.

Do these prices include shipping?

No. All figures are ex-works Wenzhou. Freight depends on destination, weight and whether you go courier, air or sea, so it is quoted separately.

How long does production take?

Samples in 5–7 days, bulk production typically 10–15 days after sample approval.
