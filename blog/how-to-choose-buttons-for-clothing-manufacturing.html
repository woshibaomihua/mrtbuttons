How to Choose Buttons for Clothing Manufacturing

A practical OEM button sourcing guide for apparel brands: choose the right button material, size, finish, testing and RFQ details for shirts, suits, coats, denim and sportswear.

Quick answer: choose garment buttons by matching fabric weight, buttonhole size, wash process, branding method and sewing equipment. For most apparel programs, start with resin buttons for color matching, corozo or horn-effect buttons for premium natural looks, metal buttons for blazer or uniform branding, and tack buttons for denim.

1. Start with the garment type

Button sourcing should begin with how the garment will be worn and washed. Shirts need thin, machine-sewable 4-hole buttons. Coats need thicker rim or shank buttons that feel substantial. Jeans need tack buttons and rivets that survive washing, abrasion and pulling force.

2. Match material to brand positioning

For price-sensitive programs, polyester resin is usually the best starting point. It supports Pantone dye-to-match, pearlescent effects, horn patterns and logo engraving. For premium collections, corozo, shell and horn-effect buttons create a more natural appearance. Metal buttons are best when the button is part of the brand identity, such as blazer crests or uniform programs.

3. Confirm buttonhole and sewing method

A beautiful button can fail if it is too thick for the machine or too wide for the buttonhole. Ask the sewing factory whether the button will be machine sewn, hand sewn, riveted, or attached with a tack. Share the buttonhole length and fabric thickness when requesting samples.

4. Specify testing before bulk production

For export apparel, buyers often request washing tests, nickel-free plating, needle detection, colorfastness or pull-force checks. Put those standards into the RFQ before sampling so the right material and finish are selected from the start.

Internal RFQ checklist

  • Product photo or tech pack
  • Button size in ligne or millimeters
  • Material target: resin, corozo, shell, metal, snap, denim hardware
  • Color reference or Pantone code
  • Logo file if branding is needed
  • Estimated quantity and destination market
  • Required testing: wash, nickel-free, REACH, pull force or needle detection

Frequently asked questions

What is the best button material for clothing manufacturing?

For most apparel programs, polyester resin is the most flexible because it supports color matching, horn effects, pearl effects and custom logo engraving. Use metal for uniforms and blazers, corozo or shell for premium natural looks, and tack buttons for denim.

What information should I send to get an accurate button quote?

Send the garment type, button photo or drawing, size, material, color, logo file, quantity, packaging requirements and destination market. If you have a tech pack, attach it to the RFQ form.

How many samples should a buyer request before bulk production?

Request at least one approved size and color sample, and for custom logo buttons request a pre-production sample before bulk. For garment programs, test the button on the actual fabric and buttonhole.
